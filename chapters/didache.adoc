== The Didache
_The Teaching of the Twelve Apostles_

[.lead]
The Didache (“The Teaching”) is the earliest surviving Christian community manual, likely dating to the late first or early second century. It was lost for centuries and rediscovered in 1873 in a monastery in Constantinople.

It opens with the “Two Ways” — the Way of Life and the Way of Death — and provides practical instructions for baptism, fasting, prayer, and community life. It contains a version of the Lord’s Prayer and instructions for the Eucharist that predate all known liturgies.

The Didache is remarkable for what it does not contain: no atonement theology, no Pauline framework, no cosmic Christ. Its Christianity is purely ethical — a community trying to live by Jesus’s actual teaching.

_Roberts-Donaldson translation (1885) — public domain._

=== Chapter 1

^1^ There are two ways,
^2^ one of life and one of death, but a great difference between the two ways. The
^3^ way of life, then, is this: First, you shall love God who made you; second,
^4^ love your neighbor as yourself, and do not do to another what you would not want
^5^ done to you. And of these sayings the teaching is this: Bless those who curse
^6^ you, and pray for your enemies, and fast for those who persecute you. For what
^7^ reward is there for loving those who love you? Do not the Gentiles do the same?
^8^ But love those who hate you, and you shall not have an enemy. Abstain from
^9^ fleshly and worldly lusts. If someone strikes your right cheek, turn to him the
^10^ other also, and you shall be perfect. If someone impresses you for one mile, go
^11^ with him two. If someone takes your cloak, give him also your coat. If someone
^12^ takes from you what is yours, ask it not back, for indeed you are not able. Give
^13^ to every one who asks you, and ask it not back; for the Father wills that to all
^14^ should be given of our own blessings (free gifts). Happy is he who gives
^15^ according to the commandment, for he is guiltless. Woe to him who receives; for
^16^ if one receives who has need, he is guiltless; but he who receives not having
^17^ need shall pay the penalty, why he received and for what. And coming into
^18^ confinement, he shall be examined concerning the things which he has done, and
^19^ he shall not escape from there until he pays back the last penny. And also
^20^ concerning this, it has been said, Let your alms sweat in your hands, until you
^21^ know to whom you should give.

=== Chapter 2

^1^ And the second
^2^ commandment of the Teaching; You shall not commit murder, you shall not commit
^3^ adultery, you shall not
^4^ commit pederasty, you shall not commit fornication, you shall not steal, you
^5^ shall not practice magic, you shall not practice witchcraft, you shall not
^6^ murder a child by abortion nor kill that which is born.
^7^ You shall not covet the things of your neighbor, you shall not swear, you shall
^8^ not bear false witness,
^9^ you shall not speak evil, you shall bear no grudge. You shall not be
^10^ double-minded nor double-tongued, for to be double-tongued is a snare of death.
^11^ Your speech shall not be false, nor empty, but fulfilled by deed. You shall not
^12^ be covetous, nor rapacious, nor a hypocrite, nor evil disposed, nor haughty. You
^13^ shall not take evil counsel against your neighbor. You shall not hate any man;
^14^ but some you shall reprove, and concerning some you shall pray, and some you
^15^ shall love more than your own life.

=== Chapter 3

^1^ My child, flee from every evil thing,
^2^ and from every likeness of it. Be not prone to anger, for anger leads to
^3^ murder. Be neither jealous, nor quarrelsome, nor of hot temper, for out of all
^4^ these murders are engendered. My child, be not a lustful one. for lust leads to fornication.
^5^ Be neither a filthy talker, nor of lofty eye, for out of all these adulteries
^6^ are engendered. My child, be not an observer of omens, since it leads to
^7^ idolatry. Be neither an enchanter, nor an astrologer, nor a purifier, nor be
^8^ willing to took at these things, for out of all these idolatry is engendered. My
^9^ child, be not a liar, since a lie leads to theft. Be neither money-loving, nor
^10^ vainglorious, for out of all these thefts are engendered. My child, be not a
^11^ murmurer, since it leads the way to blasphemy. Be neither self-willed nor
^12^ evil-minded, for out of all these blasphemies are engendered.
^13^ Rather, be meek, since the meek shall inherit the earth. Be long-suffering
^14^ and pitiful and guileless and gentle and good and always trembling at the words
^15^ which you have heard. You shall not exalt yourself, nor give over-confidence to
^16^ your soul. Your soul shall not be joined with lofty ones, but with just and
^17^ lowly ones shall it have its intercourse. Accept whatever happens to you as
^18^ good, knowing that apart from God nothing comes to pass.

=== Chapter 4

^1^ My child, remember night and day him who
^2^ speaks the word of God to you, and honor him as you do the Lord. For wherever
^3^ the lordly rule is uttered, there is the Lord. And seek out day by day the faces
^4^ of the saints, in order that you may rest upon their words. Do not long for
^5^ division, but rather bring those who contend to peace. Judge righteously, and do
^6^ not respect persons in reproving for transgressions. You shall not be undecided
^7^ whether or not it shall be. Be not a stretcher forth of the hands to receive and
^8^ a drawer of them back to give. If you have anything, through your hands you
^9^ shall give ransom for your sins. Do not hesitate to give, nor complain when you
^10^ give; for you shall know who is the good repayer of the hire. Do not turn away
^11^ from him who is in want; rather, share all things with your brother, and do not
^12^ say that they are your own. For if you are partakers in that which is immortal,
^13^ how much more in things which are mortal? Do not remove your hand from your son
^14^ or daughter; rather, teach them the fear of God from their youth. Do not enjoin
^15^ anything in your bitterness upon your bondman or maidservant, who hope in the
^16^ same God, lest ever they shall fear not God who is over both; for he comes not
^17^ to call according to the outward appearance, but to them whom the Spirit has
^18^ prepared. And you bondmen shall be subject to your masters as to a type of God,
^19^ in modesty and fear. You shall hate all hypocrisy and everything which is not
^20^ pleasing to the Lord. Do not in any way forsake the commandments of the Lord;
^21^ but keep what you have received, neither adding thereto nor taking away
^22^ therefrom. In the church you shall acknowledge your transgressions, and you

=== Chapter 5

^1^ And the way of death is this: First of
^2^ all it is evil and accursed: murders, adultery, lust, fornication, thefts,
^3^ idolatries, magic arts, witchcrafts, rape, false witness, hypocrisy,
^4^ double-heartedness, deceit, haughtiness, depravity, self-will, greediness,
^5^ filthy talking, jealousy, over-confidence, loftiness, boastfulness; persecutors
^6^ of the good, hating truth, loving a lie, not knowing a reward for righteousness,
^7^ not cleaving to good nor to righteous judgment, watching not for that which is
^8^ good, but for that which is evil; from whom meekness and endurance are far,
^9^ loving vanities, pursuing revenge, not pitying a poor man, not laboring for the
^10^ afflicted, not knowing Him Who made them, murderers of children, destroyers of
^11^ the handiwork of God, turning away from him who is in want, afflicting him who
^12^ is distressed, advocates of the rich, lawless judges of the poor, utter sinners.
^13^ Be delivered, children, from all these.

=== Chapter 6

^1^ no one causes you to err from this way of the Teaching, since apart from God it
^2^ teaches you. For if you are able to bear the entire yoke of the Lord, you will
^3^ be perfect; but if you are not able to do this, do what you are able. And
^4^ concerning food, bear what you are able; but against that which is sacrificed to
^5^ idols be exceedingly careful; for it is the service of dead gods.

=== Chapter 7

^1^ And concerning baptism, baptize this way:
^2^ Having first said all these things, baptize into the name of the Father, and of
^3^ the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, in living water. But if you have no living
^4^ water, baptize into other water; and if you cannot do so in cold water, do so in
^5^ warm. But if you have neither, pour out water three times upon the head into the
^6^ name of Father and Son and Holy Spirit. But before the baptism let the baptizer
^7^ fast, and the baptized, and whoever else can; but you shall order the baptized
^8^ to fast one or two days before.

=== Chapter 8

^1^ But let not your
^2^ fasts be with the hypocrites, for they fast on the second and fifth day of the
^3^ week. Rather, fast on the fourth day and the Preparation (Friday). Do not pray
^4^ like the hypocrites, but rather as the Lord commanded in
^5^ His Gospel, like this:
^6^ Our Father who art in heaven, hallowed be Thy name. Thy kingdom come. Thy
^7^ will be done on earth, as it is in heaven. Give us today our daily (needful)
^8^ bread, and forgive us our debt as we also forgive our debtors. And bring us
^9^ not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one (or, evil); for Thine is
^10^ the power and the glory for ever..
^11^ Pray this three times each day.

=== Chapter 9

^1^ Now concerning the Eucharist, give thanks
^2^ this way. First, concerning the cup:
^3^ We thank thee, our Father, for the holy vine of David Thy servant, which
^4^ You madest known to us through Jesus Thy Servant; to Thee be the glory for
^5^ And concerning the broken bread:
^6^ We thank Thee, our Father, for the life and knowledge which You madest
^7^ known to us through Jesus Thy Servant; to Thee be the glory for ever. Even as
^8^ this broken bread was scattered over the hills, and was gathered together and
^9^ became one, so let Thy Church be gathered together from the ends of the earth
^10^ into Thy kingdom; for Thine is the glory and the power through Jesus Christ
^11^ But let no one eat or drink of your Eucharist, unless they have been baptized
^12^ into the name of the Lord; for concerning this also the Lord has said, "Give not
^13^ that which is holy to the dogs."

=== Chapter 10

^1^ But after you are filled, give
^2^ thanks this way:
^3^ We thank Thee, holy Father, for Thy holy name which You didst cause to
^4^ tabernacle in our hearts, and for the knowledge and faith and immortality,
^5^ which You modest known to us through Jesus Thy Servant; to Thee be the glory
^6^ for ever. Thou, Master almighty, didst create all things for Thy name's sake;
^7^ You gavest food and drink to men for enjoyment, that they might give thanks to
^8^ Thee; but to us You didst freely give spiritual food and drink and life
^9^ eternal through Thy Servant. Before all things we thank Thee that You are
^10^ mighty; to Thee be the glory for ever. Remember, Lord, Thy Church, to deliver
^11^ it from all evil and to make it perfect in Thy love, and gather it from the
^12^ four winds, sanctified for Thy kingdom which Thou have prepared for it; for
^13^ Thine is the power and the glory for ever. Let grace come, and let this world
^14^ pass away. Hosanna to the God (Son) of David! If any one is holy, let him
^15^ come; if any one is not so, let him repent. Maranatha. Amen.
^16^ But permit the prophets to make Thanksgiving as much as they desire.

=== Chapter 11

^1^ therefore, comes and teaches you all these things that have been said before,
^2^ receive him. But if the teacher himself turns and teaches another doctrine to
^3^ the destruction of this, hear him not. But if he teaches so as to increase
^4^ righteousness and the knowledge of the Lord, receive him as the Lord. But
^5^ concerning the apostles and prophets, act according to the decree of the Gospel.
^6^ Let every apostle who comes to you be received as the Lord. But he shall not
^7^ remain more than one day; or two days, if there's a need. But if he remains
^8^ three days, he is a false prophet. And when the apostle goes away, let him take
^9^ nothing but bread until he lodges. If he asks for money, he is a false prophet.
^10^ And every prophet who speaks in the Spirit you shall neither try nor judge; for
^11^ every sin shall be forgiven, but this sin shall not be forgiven. But not every
^12^ one who speaks in the Spirit is a prophet; but only if he holds the ways of the
^13^ Lord. Therefore from their ways shall the false prophet and the prophet be
^14^ known. And every prophet who orders a meal in the Spirit does not eat it, unless
^15^ he is indeed a false prophet. And every prophet who teaches the truth, but does
^16^ not do what he teaches, is a false prophet. And every prophet, proved true,
^17^ working unto the mystery of the Church in the world, yet not teaching others to
^18^ do what he himself does, shall not be judged among you, for with God he has his
^19^ judgment; for so did also the ancient prophets. But whoever says in the Spirit,
^20^ Give me money, or something else, you shall not listen to him. But if he tells
^21^ you to give for others' sake who are in need, let no one judge him.

=== Chapter 12

^1^ But receive everyone who comes in
^2^ the name of the Lord, and prove and know him afterward; for you shall have
^3^ understanding right and left. If he who comes is a wayfarer, assist him as far
^4^ as you are able; but he shall not remain with you more than two or three days,
^5^ if need be. But if he wants to stay with you, and is an artisan, let him work
^6^ and eat. But if he has no trade, according to your understanding, see to it
^7^ that, as a Christian, he shall not live with you idle. But if he wills not to
^8^ do, he is a Christ-monger. Watch that you keep away from such.

=== Chapter 13

^1^ But every true prophet who wants to
^2^ live among you is worthy of his support. So also a true teacher is himself
^3^ worthy, as the workman, of his support. Every first-fruit, therefore, of the
^4^ products of wine-press and threshing-floor, of oxen and of sheep, you shall take
^5^ and give to the prophets, for they are your high priests. But if you have no
^6^ prophet, give it to the poor. If you make a batch of dough, take the first-fruit
^7^ and give according to the commandment. So also when you open a jar of wine or of
^8^ oil, take the first-fruit and give it to the prophets; and of money (silver) and
^9^ clothing and every possession, take the first-fruit, as it may seem good to you,
^10^ and give according to the commandment.

=== Chapter 14

^1^ But every Lord's day gather
^2^ yourselves together, and break bread, and give thanksgiving after having
^3^ confessed your transgressions, that your sacrifice may be pure. But
^4^ let no one who is at odds with his fellow come together with you, until they be
^5^ reconciled, that your sacrifice may not be profaned. For this is that which was
^6^ spoken by the Lord: "In every place and time offer to me a pure sacrifice; for I
^7^ am a great King, says the Lord, and my name is wonderful among the nations."

=== Chapter 15

^1^ therefore, for yourselves, bishops and deacons worthy of the
^2^ Lord, men meek, and not lovers of money, and truthful and proved; for they also
^3^ render to you the service of prophets and teachers. Therefore do not despise
^4^ them, for they are your honored ones, together with the prophets and teachers.
^5^ And reprove one another, not in anger, but in peace, as you have it in the
^6^ Gospel. But to anyone that acts amiss against another, let no one speak, nor let
^7^ him hear anything from you until he repents. But your prayers and alms and all
^8^ your deeds so do, as you have it in the Gospel of our Lord.

=== Chapter 16

^1^ Watch for your
^2^ life's sake. Let not your lamps be quenched, nor your loins unloosed; but be
^3^ ready, for you know not the hour in which our Lord will come. But come together
^4^ often, seeking the things which are befitting to your souls: for the whole time
^5^ of your faith will not profit you, if you are not made perfect in the last time.
^6^ For in the last days false prophets and corrupters shall be multiplied, and the
^7^ sheep shall be turned into wolves, and love shall be turned into hate; for when
^8^ lawlessness increases, they shall hate and persecute and betray one another, and
^9^ then shall appear the world-deceiver as Son of
^10^ God, and shall do signs and wonders, and the earth shall be delivered into his
^11^ hands, and he shall do iniquitous things which have never yet come to pass since
^12^ the beginning. Then shall the creation of men come into the fire of trial, and
^13^ many shall be made to stumble and shall perish; but those who endure in their
^14^ faith shall be saved from under the curse itself. And then shall appear the
^15^ signs of the truth: first, the sign of an outspreading in heaven, then the sign
^16^ of the sound of the trumpet. And third, the resurrection of the dead
^17^ -- yet not of all, but as it is said: "The Lord shall come and all His saints
^18^ with Him." Then shall the world see the Lord coming upon the clouds of heaven.
