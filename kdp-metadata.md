## Book Description

**Yeshu min Natzrat — "Jesus of Nazareth" in his own Aramaic.**

What did Jesus actually teach?

Not the cosmic Christ of Paul's letters. Not the miracle-worker of the Gospels. Not the divine saviour of the creeds. This book goes back to the earliest sources — the texts written by the people who actually knew him — and asks a simple question: what remains when you strip away two thousand years of theology?

What remains is a wisdom teacher from Galilee who pointed toward an interior Kingdom, a God of unconditional love, and care for the poor as the substance of authentic faith.

This compiled canon gathers the earliest recoverable teachings of Jesus into a single volume: the Gospel of Thomas (114 sayings with no narrative, no miracles, no resurrection), the Sermon on the Mount (the core of his ethical teaching from the Q source), the Gospel of Mary (Mary Magdalene as primary disciple), the letters of his own brothers James and Jude, the Didache (the earliest Christian community manual), and more.

Every text was selected for independence from Paul, early date, and proximity to the historical figure. Every inclusion and exclusion is explained in a substantial foreword that traces who Jesus was, who claimed authority after his death, and why the people closest to him — his twin, his brothers, his closest female disciple — told a very different story from the one the Church preserved.

This is version 1.0 of a living, open-source canon. It will evolve as scholarship advances. You can fork it, challenge it, and build your own.

All texts are public domain. All editorial content is released under CC0. The teachings of Jesus belong to everyone.
